Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug, third from the left, trains biologists in Mexico on how to increase wheat yields — part of his life-long war on hunger.

Here's a timeline of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 – Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1933 – Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota.
  • 1935 – Works in the Civilian Conservation Corps helping starving Americans.
  • 1937 – Finishes university and joins the US Forestry Service.
  • 1938 – Marries Margret Gibson; returns to school inspired by Stakman.
  • 1941 – Conducts research on DDT, glue, disinfectants.
  • 1942 – Earns Ph.D. in Genetics and Plant Pathology.
  • 1944 – Begins plant pathology program in Mexico.
  • 1945 – Learns to grow wheat twice yearly, doubling yields.
  • 1953 – Creates dwarf high‑yield wheat; Mexico becomes wheat self‑sufficient.
  • 1962 – Expands wheat production in India & Pakistan.
  • 1970 – Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1983 – Helps African nations increase grain yields.
  • 1984 – Becomes professor at Texas A&M.
  • 2005 – Advocates for GMO crops to feed the world.
  • 2009 – Passes away at age 95.
“Borlaug's life and achievement are testimony to the far‑reaching contribution that one man's intellect, persistence and vision can make to peace and progress.” — Manmohan Singh
